British tennis player Andy Murray on Monday stayed at the top of the Association of Tennis Professionals (ATP) world singles rankings with 11,540 points. (Also read: Andy Murray to return to Davis Cup action against France)
The 29-year-old Scot topped the rankings for first time in his career on November 7 and has kept the lead since, reports Efe.
The ATP rankings came with no changes this week as Serbian Novak Djokovic came in second with 9825 points, followed by Swiss Stan Wawrinka.
